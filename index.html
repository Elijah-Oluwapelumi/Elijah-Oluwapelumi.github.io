Professional Profile

Hi, I'm Oluwapelumi Elijah, a Geospatial Scientist with research interests in Geographic Information Systems (GIS), Remote Sensing, Earth Observation, Geospatial Intelligence, and Spatial Data Science. I recently graduated with a First-Class Bachelor of Technology (B.Tech.) degree in Remote Sensing and Geoscience Information System from the Federal University of Technology Akure (FUTA), Nigeria.

My work focuses on applying geospatial technologies to understand and address environmental and societal challenges through spatial analysis, satellite remote sensing, and data-driven decision-making. I am particularly interested in integrating GIS, Earth observation data, cloud-based geospatial platforms, and advanced analytical techniques to produce insights that support environmental sustainability, geospatial intelligence, and evidence-based decision-making.

My undergraduate research investigated the geospatial mapping and analysis of smuggling routes along the Saki West border in Oyo State, Nigeria. The study integrated satellite imagery, digital elevation models, land use and land cover information, road network analysis, and multi-criteria spatial analysis to identify areas vulnerable to illegal cross-border activities. This research strengthened my interest in applying geospatial science to national security, environmental management, and operational planning.

Beyond research, I enjoy developing geospatial workflows using ArcGIS Pro, Google Earth Engine, Python, SQL, and Power BI to transform complex spatial data into practical solutions. I am always interested in collaborating on innovative research and interdisciplinary projects that leverage geospatial technologies to solve real-world environmental and societal challenges.
